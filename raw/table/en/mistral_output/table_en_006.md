## 1. DEFINITION

"Contraband Goods" mean narcotics, stimulants, guns, swords, animals, plants, alcohol and cigarettes in excess quantity, and the like, the possession and transaction of which are prohibited by laws of the country of flag or calling port of the vessel.

## 2. PROCEDURES FOR ONBOARD SEARCH

2.1 The Master shall, as he deems necessary, search everywhere including crew cabins for concealed illegal Contraband Goods.

2.2 In case of the above, the Master shall prepare a check list for onboard search referring to the attached check list (APPENDIX-A).

2.3 The check list shall be prepared referring to the search of the following compartments:

(1) Control compartments, such as Bridge, Cargo Handling Office, and Engine Control Room.
(2) Storage space such as Bosn's Store.
(3) Cargo space in Cargo Holds or the like.
(4) Machinery space in Engine Room.
(5) Mess rooms, Galley, and Cabins in accommodation.
(6) Chain Locker, Rudder Trunk, and other spaces which the crew does not approach usually.
(7) Other places where smuggling goods are possibly concealed.

2.4 The Master shall designate crew members for the onboard search.

2.5 If deemed necessary and the Master is suspecting possible smuggling, he shall make inspection in the crew accommodation and the vessel according Apx.A.
Inspection should be done by at least 3 more persons and in the presence of the crew member.

2.6 If any Contraband Goods are discovered by the onboard search, the Master shall place such goods under his control, and report the matter to the Company immediately.

Goods in excess, such as alcohol ,cigarettes etc. including cash money, subject to Custom control by the coastal state, must be deliberately presented and declared by the crew members , to the Master for placing it, under bonded locker or Master's custody.

Non declared goods found during the onboard search ,will be considered as contraband and must be reported to Company immediately.

Note: Do not touch such discovered goods as far as possible. Drugs, in particular, must be treated very carefully, since inhalation, and only a touch to skin in an extreme case, of a drug can cause a danger to the human body.

2.7 The Master shall enter the results of the search, if carried out, in the log book.

(Example)

1000-1200 Practiced a strict ship's search for contraband goods, narcotics, and drugs. Chief Officer and designated two crewmembers rounded and inspected wholly inside of the ship, however, found no contraband goods, narcotics and drugs.

2.8 The Master shall keep on board the Check List used for the onboard search for one year.

### 3. ADDITIONAL MEASURES FOR VESSEL CALLING U.S. PORTS

The following measures shall be taken in addition to the above Articles 2 and 3.

### 3.1 Memorandum on Prevention of Drug Smuggling of U.S. Customs

The preventive measures shall be taken against drug smuggling according to the memorandum "Sea Carrier Initiative Agreement" prepared by the U.S. Customs Service based on the "Anti Drug Abuse Act 1986."

### 3.2 Time of Onboard Search

The onboard search shall be carried out without fail in one of the following cases:

(1) Just before sailing for a U.S. port (including a sailing from a U.S. port).
(2) Just before arriving at a U.S. port (including an arrival from a U.S. port).
(3) When the Master has judged that Drugs or Contraband Goods are possibly on board the vessel during navigation for a U.S. port.
