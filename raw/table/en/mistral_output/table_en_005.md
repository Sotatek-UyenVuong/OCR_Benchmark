## 1. PROCEDURES FOR PRACTICE OF ONBOARD PATROL

1.1 The Master shall prepare the Onboard Patrol Check List based on the actual conditions of the vessel with the intention of safety and security of the vessel.

1.2 The Onboard Patrol Check List shall indicate the route and items to be checked in details.

1.3 The Master shall instruct the OOW to have the deck crewmember patrol the vessel according to the Onboard Check List.

1.4 The periodical Onboard Patrol shall be carried out in Machinery Room during Unmanned Machinery Operation at night, also, in cargo holds of the laden PCC during day and night by instructions of the Master as a general rule.

1.5 The Patrol shall be carried out by a crewmembers (basically one rating crew and one officer) not on duty. Ensure that two watch keepers (one Officer and one Able Seaman) shall stand on watch on the bridge at all times to maintain safe navigation, particularly during the hours of darkness, or in poor visibility conditions. The patrol may be conducted before or after duty.

1.6 The Patrolman shall carry a transceiver, and inform the OOW of the matter immediately if he finds any abnormality.

1.7 After the Patrol, the OOW shall enter the completion of the Patrol in the Log Book. And the Check List into which the results of the Patrol have been entered shall be kept on board for one year.

## 2. MATTERS THAT REQUIRE ATTENTION OF ONBOARD PATROL

The Onboard Patrol Check List shall include the following items, which shall be paid great attention when the Onboard Patrol is carried out:

(1) check for any sign of a fire
(2) check for leakage of oil or water ,abnormal sound, smell and smoke.
(3) closure of water-tight doors.
(4) confirmation of Navigation Equipments, Steering Gear and etc. in working order.
(5) inspection of lashing conditions of movable articles on board (and stowage condition of cargo if necessary)
(6) confirm lighting condition is fully operational
(7) confirm operational telephone line of internal connections with bridge in CCR ,Steering gear etc.
